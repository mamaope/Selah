# Instructions to Counsel

## The Accountants Act, and whether Selah Solutions Ltd may lawfully exist in the shape we have designed

**Confidential · 11 July 2026 · For: [Counsel] · From: [Founder], Selah Solutions Ltd**

---

## 1. Why we are asking

We are building a Ugandan tax and compliance platform. **Before we build the part that would break the company, we would like to know whether it would.**

We have found what may be a structural problem, and we would rather be told now, plainly, than discover it after we have taken money from a client.

> **Our understanding — and it may be wrong — is that the Accountants Act requires a firm practising accountancy to be a SOLE PROPRIETORSHIP or a PARTNERSHIP, and that a LIMITED COMPANY may not hold an ICPAU practising certificate.**
>
> **Selah Solutions Ltd is a limited company.**

If that is right, then a whole tier of what we intend to do may be unlawful for us to do **in this vehicle** — and we need to know that before, not after.

**We are not asking you to bless a plan. We are asking you to break it if it is breakable.**

## 2. What we actually intend to do

Please assess each of these **separately.** They are different activities and we suspect they fall on different sides of the line.

| | What it is | Our guess |
|---|---|---|
| **A. Calculators** | A free public website that computes Ugandan tax from published law and shows its working. No client data held. No advice on a specific person's affairs. | Almost certainly unregulated. |
| **B. The Health Check** | We review a business's own records and tell them what we find — unclaimed WHT credits, arrears, a director's unfiled return blocking their TCC, reliefs they have not claimed. **We charge for this.** | 🔴 **This is the question.** Is this "accountancy practice"? |

| **F. Audit / assurance** | We will not do this. Ever. | Plainly reserved. Not our question. |

## 3. The questions, precisely

**Q1. May a limited company hold an ICPAU practising certificate?**
If not, what is the exact statutory basis, and is there any exception (e.g. a company owned wholly by certified accountants)?

**Q2. Which of activities A–E above are RESERVED** — that is, may lawfully be performed only by the holder of a practising certificate?
Please be specific about **B (the paid Health Check)** and **E (bookkeeping)**. These are the two we intend to build a business on.

**Q3. Does charging a fee change the answer?**
The same analysis, but performed free, as a lead-generation exercise — is it still reserved?

**Q4. Is TAX AGENCY genuinely separate from ACCOUNTANCY PRACTICE?**
We believe TPCA s.7(1) permits a *company* to be a registered tax agent. If that is right, is there a lawful route in which:
- **Selah Solutions Ltd** does the software, the calculators, the findings and the tax agency; and
- **a separate ICPAU-licensed firm** (a sole practice or partnership, possibly owned by our own certified accountant) does anything that is reserved?

**Q5. If such a two-vehicle structure is lawful, what must be true of it?**
Specifically: can the licensed firm be *related* to us — same founder, same premises, shared staff, a services agreement — or must it be genuinely independent? **Where is the line, and what does crossing it look like?**

**Q6. What is the CONSEQUENCE of getting this wrong?**
Is it a fine, an injunction, unenforceable contracts, personal liability for directors, or a criminal offence? **We would like to understand the downside before we take the risk, not after.**

**Q7. Is there anything else in this design that will break, that we have not thought to ask about?**
This is the most important question on the page. We are not lawyers and we do not know what we do not know.
