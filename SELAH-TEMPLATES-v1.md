## 0. Why templates matter more than they look

**The free-template business is worthless.** Anyone can google "petty cash log template." Uganda does not need another Word document.

**Four things make a Selah template different — and each one is impossible without the foundations:**

| | | Requires |
|---|---|---|
| **1. It is legally correct for Uganda, today** | Not a US or UK template with the currency changed. Sourced to the actual Act, dated, versioned. | **The engine** |
| **2. It arrives pre-filled** | Your entity details, your TIN, your directors, your employees, your ledger balances. **You do not type what Selah already knows.** | **Registry + Ledger** |
| **3. It is validated on the way out** | The engine checks the numbers *before* you submit. A trial balance that doesn't balance does not leave the building. | **The engine** |
| **4. 🔑 It tells you when it goes stale** | **This is the one nobody does.** | **Versioning** |

### The stale-template product, stated plainly

> **"Your employment contracts were drafted before the Employment (Amendment) Act 2025.**
> **Severance is now standardised at one month's salary per year worked. Casual employment is capped at six months continuous — and lay-off-and-rehire is expressly deemed continuous, so you cannot reset the clock.**
> **Your contracts do not reflect either. Here is the updated version, and here is what changed."**

**Uganda's law changes every 1 July. Every template in every business in the country goes quietly stale, and nobody is watching.**

**Selah watches.**

### F. The audit pack — *the founder's "toughest season"*

**Not a template. An assembly.** The pack pulls A1–A10 together, validated, into one deliverable the auditor can actually work from.

```
SELAH AUDIT PACK — [Entity] — FY[year]
  ├── Trial balance (validated: it balances, or it does not ship)
  ├── P&L · Balance sheet · Cash flow
  ├── Payroll register + PAYE/NSSF/LST reconciliation
  ├── Asset register + depreciation schedule
  ├── Petty cash log + receipts
  ├── Sales register + VAT reconciliation
  ├── Supplier register + TIN validation report ⚠️
  ├── WHT certificate register ⚠️ (expected vs held vs MISSING)
  ├── Bank reconciliations
  ├── Related-party transactions ⚠️ (s.115A — arm's length, NO threshold)
  └── Exceptions report — everything the engine could not reconcile
```

> 🔑 **The Exceptions Report is the most valuable page in the pack.** It is the audit finding, **found by us, in March — not by the auditor, in October.**
>
> **That is what "make audit season better" actually means.** Not a nicer spreadsheet. **The surprises removed, eight months early.**

## 3. 🔴 A template is a liability

**A wrong template is worse than no template**, because it will be used a hundred times before anyone checks it.

**Every template therefore carries the same discipline as every rule:**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Version** | `EMPLOYMENT-CONTRACT-v3 · in force from 1 July 2026` |
| **Source** | The Act and section it implements |
| **Verified date** | Stamped on the document itself |
| **Confidence** | Where a rule is uncertain, **the template says so in the template** |
| **Supersedes** | The version it replaces — **and Selah tells every client still on the old one** |
| **Reviewed** | 🔴 **Every March–June, without exception.** Uganda's amendments land 1 July. |

> **The template library is the rules engine wearing a different face.** Same versioning. Same sourcing. Same confidence ratings. **Same consequences if we get it wrong** — and under **TPCA s.69(2)**, a licensed tax agent whose template aids a tax offence pays **double the tax evaded** and faces **five years.**
