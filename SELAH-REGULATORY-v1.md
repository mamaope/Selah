# Selah — Regulatory Position

**What we may legally do, what we may not, and the one that could break the company.**

*Version 1 · 11 July 2026*

---

## 0. Read this first — I was wrong about something

Two messages ago I told you that **"options, not recommendations"** was, among other things, a regulatory shield. That *"a product that says 'here are your options, you decide' is a very different regulatory animal from one that says 'buy this'."*

**In Uganda's securities law, that is false. Comprehensively.**

**Capital Markets Authority Act, Cap 84, s.6(nn)** defines an investment adviser as a body corporate that:

> **"(i) carries on a business of advising other persons on securities; or**
> **(ii) as part of a regular business, issues or publishes, analyses or reports on securities"**

**Read limb (ii) again. It contains:**
- ❌ **no personalisation element** — generic content is caught
- ❌ **no recommendation element** — neutral analysis is caught
- ❌ **no remuneration element** — free content is caught
- ❌ **no "holding out" requirement** — you needn't call yourself an adviser

> **The statute does not care whether we recommend, personalise, or charge.**
> **It cares that we are a body corporate publishing analysis on securities as part of a business.**

**And the newspaper carve-out proves it.** Parliament wrote an *express* exemption for newspapers — and condition (ac) of that exemption is that the newspaper **receives no commission or other consideration.** Being unpaid is not a defence to the offence; **it is a condition of an exemption that applies only to newspapers.**

**Penalty: 300 currency points (~UGX 6,000,000) and/or THREE YEARS' imprisonment** (s.30(7)).

**The design principle is still right — for product, ethics, and liability under the tax-agent regime.** It is simply **not a securities shield**, and I should not have implied it was.

## 1. 🔴 THE ONE THAT COULD BREAK THE COMPANY

### The Accountants Act — and a structural problem no amount of compliance can cure

**Confidence: A on the structure. F on whether it bites us.**

| | |
|---|---|
| **s.35(2)** | Practising accountancy without a certificate → **UGX 10,000,000** and/or **2 years 10 months' imprisonment** |
| **s.1, s.31(1)** | 🔴 **An ICPAU "accounting firm" must be a SOLE PROPRIETORSHIP OR PARTNERSHIP.** |
| | 🔴 **A LIMITED COMPANY CANNOT HOLD THE LICENCE AT ALL.** |

> **If bookkeeping requires ICPAU licensing, Selah Solutions Ltd cannot fix it by getting licensed. There is no licence available to us.**
>
> **This is a structural problem, not a compliance checkbox.**

**Whether bookkeeping counts turns on the Council's definition of "accounting practices" (s.34(1)(b)) — regulations we could not locate.** But **ICPAU's own public position is expansive**: anyone offering accountancy services needs a Certificate of Practice *and* a firm licence.

### What this does and does not threaten

| | Status |
|---|---|
| **Tax agent** (TPCA ss.6–12) | ✅ **SAFE. A company CAN be a registered tax agent.** Our confirmed path stands, untouched. |
| **Radar** — arrears, WHT credits, TCC | ✅ Safe — tax-agent scope |
| **Optimise** — structuring | ✅ Probably safe — tax structuring, not accountancy practice |
| **Calculators** | ✅ Safe |
| 🔴 **Bookkeeping** | 🔴 **UNKNOWN. Potentially unavailable to a limited company, in any form.** |
| 🔴 **The pooled accountant bench** | 🔴 **Same question.** |
| 🔴 **Audit-ready / final accounts** | 🔴 **Same question.** |

**Possible structures if it does bite** *(for counsel, not for us to choose):*
1. A **partnership or sole proprietorship** affiliate that holds the ICPAU licence, with Selah Ltd as its technology provider
2. Selah **never touches the books** — it provides tools, and licensed firms use them *(the "arm the bench" model, which we already flagged as a distribution strategy)*
3. Bookkeeping turns out not to be a reserved activity at all

> ### 🔴 ACTION ZERO: get a written Ugandan counsel opinion on the Accountants Act **before anything else in this document.**
> **If bookkeeping by a limited company is unlawful and uncurable, several phases of the roadmap are moot — and we need to know that now, not in month eight.**

## 3. CMA — and the word that saves us

### 🔑 The escape hatch is "SECURITIES"

**Cap 84 s.6(hh) — this is where the product lives or dies:**

| Instrument | A security? | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Bank savings account / fixed deposit** | ✅ **NO — expressly EXCLUDED** *("certificates of deposit issued by a bank... licensed under the FIA")* | **A** |
| **Treasury BOND** | 🔴 **YES — expressly included** | **A** |
| **Treasury BILL (364-day)** | 🔴 **Probably yes — contestable, but do not bet the company on it** | **C** |
| Shares, unit trusts, CIS units | 🔴 YES | **A** |

> **The clean internal rule:**
> ### **Savings accounts and bank deposits = free. T-bills, bonds, shares, unit trusts = CMA perimeter. No exceptions.**

*(On T-bills: there is a real textual argument they're outside — a T-bill is a discount instrument, and the Act excludes "promissory notes." But limb (vi) catches instruments **"commonly known as securities"**, and in Uganda T-bills are bought through **CMA-licensed brokers**, held in the **CSD**, and **CMA itself publishes investor education called "Government Securities: A Guide to Treasury Bills and Bonds."** **Assume they are securities.**)*

### CMA is not a dormant regulator

Its standing public notice warns against *"individuals or firms holding out as investment advisers... operating physically and **through online platforms**"* and confirms breach of ss.19 and 30 is **liable to prosecution.** It ran *"CMA Advances Crackdown on Investment Scams"* in July 2025 and maintains a Criminal Proceedings enforcement function.

### ⭐ And the route through: the licence is cheap, and the sandbox is open

| | |
|---|---|
| **Who may hold it** | **A body corporate.** ✅ Selah qualifies. |
| **Application fee** | **UGX 250,000** |
| **Broker/dealer capital rules (UGX 5m deposit, UGX 15m net capital)** | ✅ **Do NOT apply to investment advisers** |
| **Minimum paid-up capital for an adviser** | **Possibly none. UNCONFIRMED — verify.** |
| **Decision timeline** | **45 days** (reg. 13, SI 84-1) |
| **Licensed investment advisers in Uganda** | **Seven.** |

> 🔑 **The investment adviser licence appears to be, by an order of magnitude, the cheapest financial licence in this entire brief** — against an insurance broker at UGX 75m capital + 19m deposit + 100m PI.
>
> **If securities content matters to Selah, LICENSING may be dramatically more achievable than avoiding the perimeter.**

### ⭐⭐ The CMA Regulatory Sandbox — launched October 2025, with about four applicants

CMA published formal **Sandbox Rules** in October 2025: *"a safe, supervised environment for fintech innovators to test new products and business models under CMA's oversight."* There is a published **Call for Expression of Interest** and a **Regulatory Sandbox Checklist**.

> **This is new, under-subscribed, and precisely designed for an entity in Selah's position. It is the cheapest available path to certainty — and to a relationship with the regulator before we need one.**

## 4. The other three, in brief

### URBRA — ✅ we are free
- URBRA licenses **schemes, trustees, administrators, fund managers, custodians**. **There is NO adviser, consultant, broker or intermediary category.**
- **s.73(2) expressly contemplates unlicensed expert advisers** — a fiduciary *"may obtain expert advice from actuaries, lawyers, auditors, investment advisers, financial experts..."*
- ⚠️ **The real risk is functional drift.** If Selah performs **s.53 administrator functions** — keeping scheme records, processing receipts, producing accounts, preparing benefit statements — it needs an **administrator licence**. **UGX 20m and/or 6 years.** Draw that line in the contract.
- ⭐ **The right product is the UMBRELLA SCHEME route:** an SME joins an existing licensed umbrella scheme by **deed of adherence**. No scheme to establish, no providers to appoint, no licence, no fee. **Ten licensed umbrella schemes exist.**
- 🔴 **Correction to our structuring guide:** the employer/employee asymmetry is confirmed, and it matters. **The employee's own contribution is NOT deductible.** The efficiency exists **only** on the employer-contribution route. We had this right — but the guide must state it unmissably.

### UMRA — lending
- Money lender licence: **company only**, UGX 50,000 application, **UGX 500,000/year**, **no minimum capital**, **expires 31 December annually**.
- ⚠️ **Interest cap: 2.8%/month = 33.6% p.a.** (Legal Notice 21 of 2024). Applies to money lenders; apparently **not** to NDTMFIs. **The licence class is a strategic choice.**
- ✅ **Best news in the brief: there is NO credit-intermediary, loan-broker or introducer licence in Uganda.** The old Moneylenders Act criminalised paid loan introducers; the Tier 4 Act 2016 repealed it and **did not re-enact it.**
- 🔴 **BUT s.96: it is illegal to charge the BORROWER any fee "incidental to or relating to the negotiations for the granting of the loan."** **Commission must be paid by the LENDER, out of its own margin, and must never appear in borrower-facing pricing.**
- 🔴 **Credit scoring:** running a *bureau* is licensable (**UGX 5m and/or 2 years**, UGX 200m capital). Scoring internally, or bilaterally for one lender, is **arguably** outside — **untested**. ✅ **Clean route: reg. 39(8) lets a data subject authorise a third party in writing to obtain their credit report from a CRB. Use that.**
- 🚩 **THE SLEEPER RISK — payment initiation.** BoU's *NPS Oversight Policy Framework* (Aug 2025) §2.3(d): providers that *"provide the enabling technology to support funds transfers"* and *"do not provide consumer-facing services"* **are also subject to licensing as PSOs.** **"We never touch the money" is not a defence — it is the definition of the bucket.** Body-corporate penalty: **UGX 140,000,000 + permanent disqualification.** *(Note: this is consistent with our existing hard rule — Selah never holds client tax money. Keep it, and structure any payment flow strictly as a contracted vendor to a licensee.)*

### IRA — insurance
- **"Insurance broker"** includes one who *"advises an insured or prospective insured on his or her insurance needs and requirements"* — **advising alone is enough.** Remuneration is the gateway.
- 🚩 **BUT "risk advisor" = "a person who, by way of business, assesses and advises on insurable risks" — with NO remuneration element.** *"We take no commission"* is **not a complete defence.**
- 🚩 **s.82(2)(b): merely HOLDING OUT** — any name, title or description implying you are an intermediary — **is itself sufficient.** **Never label anything "insurance advisor," "risk advisor," or "insurance broker."**
- 🔴 **A commission-based multi-insurer marketplace requires a BROKER licence** — an agent is tied to one insurer per class. **UGX 75m paid-up capital + UGX 19m deposit + UGX 100m professional indemnity.**
- 🔴 **s.90: premium may NEVER be electronically transferred into a broker's or agent's account, including a client account.**
- **No aggregator carve-out, no insurtech licence, no price-comparison exemption exists in Uganda.**

## 5. 🔴 DATA PROTECTION — the root-level problem

**This one is not about a feature. It is about whether the business is lawful at all.**

> ### **DPPA s.9(1): *"A person shall NOT collect or process personal data which relates to... financial information..."***

**Uganda treats financial data as SPECIAL personal data — unlike GDPR.** Selah's entire product is built on data whose processing is **prohibited by default.**

**The s.7(2) exceptions (contract necessity, legitimate interest) do NOT carry across to special data.** The realistic gateway is **s.9(3)(b) — explicit, granular, unbundled, opt-in consent.**

> **Get the consent architecture right, or the business is unlawful at the root.**
>
> *(This dovetails with what we already designed: consent is per-taxpayer, and a company's appointment does not grant access to its directors' personal ledgers. That instinct was right. It now has a statutory basis — and a criminal penalty behind it.)*

| Obligation | Detail |
|---|---|
| 🔴 **PDPO registration** | **MANDATORY. Universal. No threshold.** UGX 100,000/year. |
| 🔴 **Enforcement is LIVE and CRIMINAL** | **First conviction: 10 July 2025 — a director of a digital lender, personally convicted, for failure to register.** Reg 15(4) attaches liability to **"every officer of the corporation."** A **free public register** exists — our absence from it is trivially provable. |
| 🔴 **DPO** | **MANDATORY for Selah** — core activities consist of processing special personal data |
| **Breach notification** | **"IMMEDIATELY."** There is no 72-hour grace. |
| **Cross-border** | Adequacy **AND** consent (satisfy both). Onward transfer to a third country — e.g. a cloud host, or an AI API — needs its **own express consent**. **UGX 40,000 PER DAY, uncapped.** |
| **Corporate fine** | On conviction, up to **2% of ANNUAL GROSS TURNOVER**. Sections carry **10-year maxima.** |
| 🚩 **s.27 — automated decisions** | A data subject may require by written notice that no decision significantly affecting them be made **solely** by automated processing. **An automated credit score deciding who gets a loan is squarely within this. Build the human-in-the-loop override now.** |

✅ **Two things we feared are NOT true:** there is **no banking-secrecy bar** in the Financial Institutions Act, and **tax secrecy (TPCA s.55) binds tax OFFICERS, not tax agents.** It does not bind Selah.

## 6. Feature by feature — the verdict

| | Feature | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **a** | Free calculator: **T-bill yields + their tax treatment** | ⚠️ **SPLIT — REDESIGN.** The **tax half is legal** (tax-agent scope). The **yield half is inside the CMA perimeter.** A tool where *the user inputs their own rate* is defensible; one that **publishes current rates and models returns** looks like "issuing analyses on securities." |
| **b** | Retirement projection calculator | ✅ **LEGAL.** URBRA licenses no advisers. **Guardrail: never model NAMED funds' expected returns — that flips it into CMA.** |
| **c** | Salary vs dividend vs retain, priced, with trade-offs | ✅ **LEGAL** — tax structuring, squarely within tax-agent scope. *(Subject to §1.)* |
| **d** | *"Your surplus cash could earn X% in a 364-day T-bill vs Y% in a savings account"* | 🔴 **MUST BE REDESIGNED. The clearest red flag in the brief.** Savings account = not a security. **T-bill = security.** Comparative returns = limb (ii). *"YOUR surplus cash"* = limb (i). **Our "options" framing gives us NOTHING here. Three years' imprisonment.** |
| **e** | Marketplace → accountants, lawyers, auditors (**commission**) | ❓ **UNCLEAR — RESEARCH GAP.** Not a CMA/IRA/UMRA issue. **But the Advocates Act / Law Council rules on fee-sharing and touting, and ICPAU rules on referral commissions, were NOT researched — and both commonly prohibit paying non-professionals for referrals.** |
| **f** | Marketplace → insurance or investment providers (**commission**) | 🔴 **NEEDS A LICENCE, both legs.** Insurance: **broker licence** (UGX 75m+). Investments: commission for introducing clients risks the **"representative"** category. |
| **g** | Underwrite/score a business for **the founder's own lending arm** | ⚠️ **REDESIGN — HIGHEST-RISK ITEM.** No licence for scoring per se, **but**: the Tier 4 Act's "moneylender" definition is circular, and the related-party flow (Selah holds the data → Selah scores → Selah refers only to the founder's lender → Selah takes a fee) is **exactly the fact pattern that collapses corporate separation.** Plus DPPA s.27 and s.9. **Mitigations:** genuine independent credit decision at the lender; score advisory, not determinative; **no common directors on the credit committee**; **non-exclusive** referrals; borrower pays Selah nothing (s.96). **Get a written opinion on this structure specifically.** |
| **h** | Recommend an employer retirement contribution scheme | ✅ **LEGAL.** **Guardrails:** never perform s.53 administrator functions; steer SMEs to an **umbrella scheme via deed of adherence**; only **employer** contributions are efficient; if we take provider commission, that's a **conflict** issue — disclose or refuse. |

## 7. What we ship, and what we hold back

### ✅ Ship now — entirely outside every perimeter
- **All tax calculators, Radar, Optimise, the filing calendar, TCC readiness** *(tax-agent scope)*
- **Savings-account content** — bank deposits are **expressly excluded** from "securities"
- **Retirement projections and employer-contribution structuring** — URBRA licenses no advisers
- **Budgeting, forecasting, bookkeeping tools** *(subject to §1)*

### 🔴 Hold back until the regulator answers
- **Any modelling of T-bill, bond, share or unit-trust returns**
- **Any comparison of a security against anything**
- **Any insurance comparison or recommendation**
- **Any commission-based financial-product marketplace**

### The line, in one sentence

> **We may say what the law is, what the tax is, and what a bank deposit pays.**
> **We may not say what a security will earn — however carefully we phrase it.**
