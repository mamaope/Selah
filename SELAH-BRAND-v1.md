# Selah — Brand Identity

*Version 1 — 11 July 2026*

---

## 1. The name

**"Selah" (Hebrew: סֶלָה) appears 71 times in the Psalms and 3 times in Habakkuk.**

**Its meaning is genuinely disputed.** Scholars offer two main readings:

| Reading | Basis |
|---|---|
| **A pause** — a musical or literary interlude; a cue to *stop, consider, and weigh what has just been said* | The dominant liturgical interpretation |
| **"To lift up"** — praise, elevate | From the probable Hebrew root **סָלָה** *(salah)* |

*Sources: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selah) · [Logos](https://www.logos.com/grow/bsm-what-does-selah-mean/) · [Ligonier](https://learn.ligonier.org/articles/what-selah-means-in-the-bible)*

### 🔑 Both readings are the company. And so is the uncertainty.

**The pause** is the thesis. Every founding story is a failure to pause:

- Five years of invoices, and nobody paused to check the certificates. **UGX 70,000,000.**
- A grant ended, and nobody paused to check the payroll. **NSSF arrears, still unresolved.**
- An audit arrived, and nobody had paused all year. **Nothing reconciled.**

**"To lift up"** is what happens after the pause. You see where you stand — and then you rise.

> ### **Selah. Pause — and know where you stand.**

### And here is the part that matters most

**We will not pretend the etymology is settled. It isn't.**

**A company whose entire brand promise is *"we tell you what we don't know"* cannot overclaim the meaning of its own name.** When we tell the story publicly, we say: *the scholars disagree; the most common reading is "pause and consider"; here is why we chose it anyway.*

> **If we will be honest about our own name, a Ugandan business owner might believe us about their tax.**
>
> **That is not a clever line. That is the entire strategy.**

## 2. Positioning

> **Selah tells Ugandan businesses and individuals what they owe, what they're owed, and what they can legally keep — before URA tells them.**

**One line:** *Pause — and know where you stand.*

**The story, in a paragraph:**

> Uganda's tax rules are public, but they are not knowable. They change every July. Right now, the Uganda Revenue Authority's own website publishes the wrong PAYE bands. PwC's published guidance states the wrong VAT threshold. A three-year tax exemption for new businesses sits in the gazette, absent from URA's own exemptions page.
>
> **In this environment, a business does not fail to comply because it is dishonest. It fails because nobody can tell it what the number is.**
>
> **Selah is the one who checked.**

## 3. The brand posture

### Reject the default.

The instinctive aesthetic for an African fintech is bright, warm, optimistic — gradients, illustrated people, rounded everything, an exclamation mark. **That aesthetic signals *app*. We must signal *authority*.**

**Our reference points are not fintech. They are: a law report. A gazette. A well-set reference book. An audit opinion.**

Sober. Precise. Dense with citation. Quietly certain. **Something you would be comfortable printing and handing to a URA officer.**

| Selah IS | Selah is NOT |
|---|---|
| Precise | Playful |
| Sourced | Salesy |
| Sober | Cold |
| Certain where it can be — **honest where it can't** | Confident about everything |
| Plain | Simplistic |
| **The one who checked** | The one who guessed prettily |

## 4. The six principles

**These are public commitments, not internal values. They are testable, and customers can hold us to them.**

| # | | In the product |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **We show our working.** | Every number, three levels deep: the answer, the working, the law |
| **2** | **We date everything.** | *"Verified 11 July 2026. Uganda's tax law changes every 1 July."* |
| **3** | **We say what we don't know.** | Confidence C → **the calculator refuses, and explains why** |
| **4** | **We never say "do this."** | Options, priced, with trade-offs. **The decision is yours.** |
| **5** | **We cite.** | Act, section, gazette. **Never a blog.** |
| **6** | **We show the conflict.** | *"URA's page says X. The gazetted Act says Y. Here is both."* |

> **The logo is downstream of these six lines.**

## 5. Voice

**Plain, precise, unhurried. Never breathless. Never patronising. The numbers do the persuading — we get out of the way.**

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---|---|
| *"Unlock massive tax savings!"* | *"You may not have owed the tax you paid. Here's how to check."* |
| *"Easily manage your compliance."* | *"URA is holding UGX 3,600,000 that belongs to you. You hold 4 of 11 certificates."* |
| *"Our AI-powered engine..."* | *"We read the gazetted Act. URA's website hasn't been updated yet."* |
| *"Consult a professional."* | *"We haven't seen your contracts. Ask your adviser whether this applies to you."* |
| *"Get started today!"* | *"Shall we check your last six payslips?"* |

**Rules:**
- **Sentence case.** Always.
- **No exclamation marks.** Ever.
- **No emoji** in product or public copy.
- **Contractions are fine.** We are plain, not stiff.
- **Never "simply," "just," "easy."** They condescend, and tax is not easy.
- **Numbers are always specific.** *"UGX 3,600,000"*, never *"thousands."*
- **Every claim carries a date.**

## 6. Visual identity

### The mark

> ### **‖**

**A caesura — the pause mark.** In music and in verse, the double bar means *stop here.*

It is the name, rendered. It is the product, rendered. **It is a mark you can set in a line of type, stamp on a document, and put in the corner of a letter to URA without embarrassment.** It does not look like an app icon, and that is the point.

**Wordmark:** `‖ SELAH` — the mark, a space, the name. Set in the serif. Never distorted, never gradient, never animated.

### Colour

**Ink on paper, with one accent and three signals.**

| Role | | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Ink** | Near-black, warm | Text, the wordmark. The default. |
| **Paper** | Warm off-white | Background. Not clinical white — **paper**. |
| **Accent — deep teal** | `#0F6E56` | The one colour. Considered, not excited. Used sparingly: the mark, key actions, the engine. |
| **Verified** | Green | Confidence A. Money you're owed. |
| **Caution** | Amber | Confidence B/C. Conflicts with official sources. Deadlines approaching. |
| **Stop** | Red | Confidence F — a refusal. Money you owe. Arrears. |

**No gradients. No shadows. No illustration. Hairline rules, generous white space, and numbers that line up.**

### Type

| | | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Serif** — headlines, the wordmark, the voice | A reference-book serif | **Authority.** This is a document, not a dashboard. |
| **Sans** — UI, labels, body | Clean, neutral, high-legibility | Gets out of the way |
| 🔑 **Mono — every number** | A tabular monospace | **This is the most important typographic decision in the brand.** |

> **Numbers are always monospaced and right-aligned, so decimals stack.**
>
> It signals *figures, not marketing.* It is what a ledger looks like, what an audit schedule looks like, what a gazette looks like. **Set a tax computation in a proportional font and it reads like a blog post. Set it in mono and it reads like a fact.**

## 7. 🔑 The trust system

**This is the load-bearing part of the brand. Everything above is preparation for these six components.**

| Component | What it does | Why it's brand, not UI |
|---|---|---|
| **The confidence badge** | `A` primary law · `B` corroborated · `C` uncertain · `F` unknown | **We publish our own uncertainty.** Nobody else does. |
| **The verified stamp** | *"Verified 11 July 2026"* | **An undated tax claim is a liability.** The stamp is our signature. |
| **The citation block** | Act · section · gazette · link | **We cite primary law. Never a blog.** |
| **The working** | Band by band, on the user's own numbers | *"Show your working"* — the oldest trust device there is |
| 🔑 **The refusal card** | *"We cannot compute this, and we will not guess. Here is why."* | **Every competitor returns a number here. We return the truth.** This is the single most persuasive screen in the product. |
| **The options table** | Routes, priced, with what each requires of you | **We never say "do this."** |

> **A competitor can copy our calculators in a weekend.**
> **They cannot copy a refusal card, because to publish one you have to have actually checked — and then been willing to say you don't know.**

## 8. Naming

| Working name | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **Radar** | 🟡 Fine, but generic. **What does a Ugandan business owner actually call this?** *"The check."* *"The health check."* Test it in Phase 1 — the customers will name it. |
| **Optimise** | 🔴 **Weak, and slightly wrong.** It sounds like a growth hack, and it is the module with criminal exposure. Consider something plainer and more honest: **"What you can keep."** |
| **The Calculator Centre** | ✅ Plain and accurate. Keep. |
| **The Health Check** | ✅ **Strong.** Medical, sober, and it implies a professional did it. |

> **Do not finalise product names before Phase 1. Twenty paying customers will name these products better than we can.**

## 9. What this brand forbids

**Written down so it cannot be quietly abandoned when someone wants a growth spurt.**

- ❌ **Never a number without a source.**
- ❌ **Never a claim without a date.**
- ❌ **Never "do this."**
- ❌ **Never quietly override an official source.** Show the conflict.
- ❌ **Never compute-and-disclaim.** If we don't know, we refuse.
- ❌ **Never an exclamation mark.**
- ❌ **Never a testimonial in place of a citation.**
- ❌ **Never a growth tactic that costs us a shred of the thing we are actually selling.**

> **We are selling the belief that we checked. Everything else is downstream of that, and nothing is worth spending it on.**
