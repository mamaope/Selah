# Memorandum of Understanding — Payment by Instalments

**Selah · v1 · Verified 11 July 2026**

---

## 🔑 Why this document unlocks the tender

URA's own Tax Clearance Certificate criteria, criterion 5:

> *"All taxes due are paid, **OR there is a Memorandum of Understanding to pay in instalments**."*

**Read that again.** You do not need to have paid. **You need an agreed plan.**

The Commissioner General has publicly confirmed that distressed businesses are *"not automatically disqualified"*. A company with an arrear it cannot clear today, and an MOU it is honouring, **can hold a valid Tax Clearance Certificate.**

Almost no Ugandan business owner knows this. They assume the arrear is a wall. **It is a door, and this is the key.**

**Sequence matters:**

> **Disclose → agree the MOU → get the TCC → win the tender → pay the instalments out of the contract.**

## Notes for whoever sends this

### Do not propose a schedule you cannot keep

**A broken MOU is worse than no MOU.** It converts a cooperative taxpayer into a defaulting one, and the enforcement that follows is not gentle. **Model the instalments against your actual cash flow, at the low end, and then add a month.**

### The undertaking that breaks people

> *"We will remain current on all NEW liabilities as they fall due."*

This is where most arrangements die. You are paying down the old arrear **and** meeting every new obligation, out of the same cash. **If that is not achievable, propose a longer schedule now** rather than a shorter one you will breach in month three.

### Attach the evidence

Management accounts. The debtors listing. The order book. **A proposal is a story; the attachments are what make it credible.** A finance officer who can see the receivable that will fund instalment three can approve instalment one.

### 🔴 And the thing nobody tells you

**A refusal is a "tax decision".**

The Tax Procedures Code Act is **completely silent** on the criteria for issuing a Tax Clearance Certificate. All five of URA's criteria are **administrative, not statutory** — which means a refusal is objectionable under **TPCA s.24** and appealable to the **Tax Appeals Tribunal.**

Most Ugandan business owners do not know that remedy exists. **You are not obliged to accept "no" as the end of the conversation.**
