# Letter — Request for Withholding Tax Certificates

**Selah · v1 · Verified 11 July 2026**

> **Send this to every client who has withheld tax from you and not given you the certificate.**
>
> It is not a complaint. It is a routine, friendly request for a document they are already required to hold — and it works, because **they are the ones exposed if they got it wrong.**

**Tone:** cooperative. You are not accusing them of anything. You are making it easy.

## Notes for whoever sends this

**Attach the schedule.** A request without a list gets ignored. A request with invoice numbers, dates and amounts gets actioned, because it can be handed to a junior and closed.

**The last paragraph is the one that works.** *"If any of these deductions were not in fact remitted…"* — said neutrally, with no accusation. **The withholding agent is personally liable for tax it deducted and failed to remit** (and for tax it should have deducted and didn't). You are quietly offering to help them find that out before URA does. Finance managers answer that letter.

**Twenty-one days, then call.** Not email. Call.

**If they refuse or go silent:** you may still claim the credit if you can evidence the deduction another way — the remittance advice, the bank statement showing a net payment against a gross invoice, the contract. It is harder, and URA may push back. **Get the certificate.**

🔴 **And if you are on the other side of this desk** — if *you* are the one who withheld — **issue the certificate.** It costs you nothing, and without it your supplier pays tax twice on money you already sent to URA. That is Isaac, from the other side of the table.
