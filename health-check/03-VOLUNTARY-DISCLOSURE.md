# Letter — Voluntary Disclosure to the Commissioner

**Selah · v1 · Verified 11 July 2026 · Tax Procedures Code Act Cap. 343**

---

## 🔴 READ THIS BEFORE YOU SEND ANYTHING

### What this is worth

Where a taxpayer **voluntarily discloses** an offence to the Commissioner **before court proceedings commence**, the Commissioner **MAY compound the offence**: the taxpayer pays the outstanding tax, and **no interest and no fine.**

**On a UGX 4,000,000 arrear that has run five years, that is UGX 9,124,123 of interest.** More than twice the tax itself.

This converts a diagnosis into a cure:

> **Find the arrear → disclose it → agree instalments → get the TCC → win the tender.**

Every step of that is in URA's own published rules. **Nobody in Uganda is selling it as a product.**

### 🔴 And what it is NOT

**THE WORD IS "MAY". NOT "MUST".**

It is a **discretion**, not an entitlement. We can tell you exactly what is at stake — that is arithmetic. **We cannot tell you how the Commissioner will exercise the discretion in your case, and anyone who tells you they can is guessing with your money.**

**Three things that must be true before you send this:**

| | |
|---|---|
| **1. It must be genuinely voluntary.** | If URA has already opened an audit, issued an assessment, or written to you about this, **you may be past the point where this is available. The door closes quietly and you are not told.** |
| **2. It is irrevocable.** | You are telling the tax authority about an offence. You cannot un-tell them. |
| **3. Do it with an adviser.** | A badly drafted disclosure **widens** the enquiry instead of closing it. This template is a starting point, not a substitute for someone who will sign their name to it. |

**Interest does not stop while you dispute.** In *URA v Airtel* the Supreme Court held that penal tax and interest continue to accrue throughout objection and Tribunal proceedings. The 30% deposit is the price of being **heard** — not a moratorium. **Waiting is the most expensive thing you can do.**

## Notes for whoever sends this

**A director signs it.** Not the bookkeeper. It signals that the company is taking it seriously, and it is the person URA will want in the room anyway.

**Do not offer a legal argument.** This is not an objection. You are not disputing the tax — you are disclosing it. Arguing about whether it is due undermines the entire premise and invites an assessment instead of a compounding.

**Compute the principal yourself, and show your working.** A disclosure with no schedule reads as a fishing expedition. A disclosure with a clean schedule reads as a company that has its house in order and made one mistake.

**Say what changed.** *"The error has been corrected"* is worth more than any apology. They are deciding whether you will be back.

🔴 **And know the number you are asking to be waived.** Run the voluntary disclosure calculator first. Walking into that meeting without knowing that the interest is UGX 9,124,123 — larger than the tax — is walking in without knowing what you are negotiating for.
